The Brief

A design studio needed an identity as deliberate as the work it produces

Forma Studio is a multidisciplinary creative practice. The brief was clear: build a visual identity that communicates precision, warmth, and craft — without veering into the cold minimalism that dominates the design industry. The name "Forma" — from the Latin for "shape" and "beauty" — set the design direction.

Logo explorations — sketch and digital
The Process

From sketch to full identity system

1 Brand discovery workshop — defining values (precise, warm, considered), target audience (architects, UI/UX teams, cultural institutions), and competitive landscape analysis.
2 Exploration of three logo directions: a geometric mark, a typographic wordmark, and a hybrid. Client selected a refined wordmark built on a custom letterform.
3 Color system developed around warm neutrals (sand, off-white, near-black) with a single accent — deep terracotta — to signal warmth without loudness.
4 Typography pairing: Fraunces for editorial headlines (voice, personality) and Inter for UI and body text (clarity, utility). Full type scale documented.
5 Identity applied across stationery, digital templates, social media, and a comprehensive brand guidelines PDF delivered in InDesign.

The Outcome

An identity that holds across every touchpoint

The delivered brand system included: primary and secondary logo lockups, a complete color palette with usage rules, a type scale, icon set, stationery suite, social media templates, and a 28-page brand guidelines document. The client used the identity immediately for a client pitch — and landed the project.
