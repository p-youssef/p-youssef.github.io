The Initiative

Making architectural knowledge accessible — one Reel at a time

Handize Architecture Academy is a volunteer-led educational platform for architects and architecture students. As Architecture Content Creator, I develop and produce Instagram Reels covering two distinct content streams: practical Adobe Illustrator tips for architects, and real-life architectural project analyses that connect built examples to design principles.

The challenge: communicate technical design knowledge through a format built for 15-second entertainment. Every video is a design problem in itself — what's the single clearest thing this piece can teach, and what's the fastest path to that insight?

Content Streams

Two formats, one mission

01 Illustrator Tips & Tricks for Architects — fast-paced screen-capture Reels demonstrating specific workflow techniques: hatching systems, line weight conventions, drawing automation, and presentation templates. Each under 60 seconds.
02 Real-Life Project Analysis — longer-form Reels (60–90 seconds) analyzing a built project's key design moves: site strategy, structural logic, section quality, material honesty. Visual annotation over architectural photography and drawings.

Process

Design the content before filming a single frame

1 Topic research and script outline — identify the single learning outcome, structure the beats, write sparse captions that supplement rather than repeat the visuals.
2 Visual preparation in Illustrator — build the demo files cleanly so the screen-capture footage tells the story without dead time or confusion.
3 Edit in CapCut — pace cuts to music, add motion graphics annotations, apply the series visual identity for consistency across episodes.
4 Review against the learning outcome — if a viewer can't identify the key takeaway in the first 3 seconds, the piece gets re-cut.
Reflection

Teaching design taught me to design better

Creating educational content forces a level of clarity that client work rarely demands. When you have to explain why a design decision works — not just show that it does — you develop a much sharper vocabulary for your own practice. The Handize series has been one of the most valuable design exercises I've undertaken.
