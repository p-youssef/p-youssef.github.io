The Concept

A civic space that bridges Damascus's layered history and its contemporary life

This academic project explored what a cultural center for Damascus could look like — a building that absorbs the city's layered identity (ancient medina, French mandate urbanism, mid-century modernism) without replicating or nostalgically quoting any single layer.

The design response: a building organized around a central courtyard — a typological root shared across Syrian architecture — with a contemporary tectonic language that uses light and material honestly.

The Process

From site analysis to detailed technical documentation

1 Site analysis — sun path, prevailing winds, topography, pedestrian flow, views. Damascus's hot-dry climate drove the courtyard typology and deep overhang strategy.
2 Programmatic organization: gallery spaces, a performance hall (250 seats), a library, workshops, café, and public roof terrace — organized to allow independent operation of each zone.
3 Structural system designed in Revit — exposed concrete frame with infill stone cladding referencing local limestone masonry.
4 Full technical drawing set in AutoCAD: site plan (1:500), floor plans (1:100), sections (1:100), elevations (1:100), and one detailed 1:20 section through the courtyard facade.
5 Photo-realistic exterior and interior renders produced in Rayon — daytime and dusk lighting scenarios to communicate atmosphere.

Reflection

Architecture as synthesis, not spectacle

The project was formally recognized by the faculty jury for the integration of passive climate strategy with spatial quality — the same courtyard that provides shade and natural ventilation also becomes the social heart of the building. The most interesting architectural problems are always the ones where performance and experience point to the same solution.
