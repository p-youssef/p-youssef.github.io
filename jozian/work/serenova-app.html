The Problem

Users were abandoning mental wellness apps before forming any habit

Mental health apps suffer from high churn — users download, engage for a few days, then disappear. Research consistently shows the main culprits are overwhelming onboarding, cluttered dashboards, and daily check-ins that take too long. Most check-ins were clocking in at over three minutes — too much friction to sustain as a daily practice.

Serenova started with a simple question: what if a daily mental health check-in felt as quick and natural as unlocking your phone?

The Process

Research-led design, tested at every stage

1 12 user interviews across age groups 18–35, focusing on daily routines, emotional check-in habits, and pain points with existing apps.
2 Affinity mapping and user journey mapping in Miro — identified three primary friction moments: long onboarding, cluttered home screens, and multi-step check-in flows.
3 Low-fidelity wireframes tested with 8 participants using think-aloud protocols. Key finding: a single-screen check-in with a mood slider plus one open-field outperformed all other formats.
4 Iterative high-fidelity UI in Figma — soft typography, a warm muted palette, and generous whitespace to signal calm rather than urgency.
5 Final usability testing across two rounds — reduced average check-in time from 3:20 to 0:45 min. Onboarding completion rate improved from 54% to 91%.

The Outcome

76% faster. A habit that finally sticks.

A streamlined onboarding flow reduced time-to-first-check-in to under 90 seconds. The redesigned single-screen daily check-in cut task completion time by 76%, while a soft warm palette rooted in calm reduced self-reported anxiety about the app itself.

The project demonstrated how rigorous UX research — not visual trends — drives meaningful product improvement. Every design decision traced back to a user insight.
